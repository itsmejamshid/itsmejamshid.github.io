It's All Bugs

October 31, 2025 3 min read
Reality Check Burnout Personal

A raw reflection on the endless cycle of software development and bugs.

after years of looking at code in a variety of languages from a variety of teams, I’ve decided:

there is no good code. there are no good languages.

all code is garbage. computers are garbage. return to monke

— Low Level (@LowLevelTweets) April 9, 2025

I don't know how to start this thought process of mine, but in short, it is all bugs.

All of the codes you wrote are vulnerability that can be exploited, and your job is to fix them until the end of time — or at least until AI reaches AGI level in the 2030s, and then your job will be in farm.

I no longer see software as something that gives user the comfortable experience, but something that can go wrong at any time. Every feature, every so-called "improvement," is just another doorway for things to break.

The Endless Cycle

Need to add new feature? Okay, let me check with the rest of service codebase.

Hold on, we can optimize this code piece — but 50 more functions depends on this one. You know what, we gonna change all of them, and we have optimized codebase, thanks to new feature.

Another feature? Oh, hold on, previous logic was the correct, we should un-optimize it again, and the rest is history.

It is just this endless repeating cycle until your codes reaches the form that barely works, and any new change or misuse can break previously thought logic, which needs to be re-optimized.

It never ends. The same loop, over and over again, like debugging a déjà vu.

The Weight of It All

I know it is all skill issue, but I don't know how to improve, and I am near another burnout.

Maybe this is just how it is — writing, rewriting, fixing, breaking, pretending it's progress.

Maybe I should start learning functional language at this point. Maybe it will fix something. Or maybe not.
